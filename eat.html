EAT

Where to Eat

  • Llama

    About: A member of the Copenhagen Food Collective, Llama is one Copenhagen's best and only Latin-American restaurants. Llama has a great menu and a great bar and it's dark but colorful atmosphere makes it a great place for a fun night out or a romantic dinner. Check it out if you're looking to spice up your Copenhagen experience.

    Info

  • Bio Mio

    About: BOB BISTRO (BioMio) is a sustainable and organic bistro in Vesterbro's meatpacking district. The perfect place for a conscious meat eater and a vegetarian, they have options for all. They've got great indoor and outdoor seating with great service. A great spot to hit up before a night on the town in one of Copenhagen's popular clubbing districts.

    Info

  • Madklubben Vesterbro

    About: Madklubben's Vesterbro branch is one of the most popular restaurants in Copenhagen but with good reason. This restaurant offers options for everyone vegetarian and vegan (which can be hard to find in Copenhagen if your don't ask) at a great price. With a bustling and vibrant interior this restaurant is a great stop for any copenhagen visitor.

    Info

  • Paté Paté

    About: A Vesterbro meatpacking favorite, Paté Paté offers French, Spanish and Moroccan food in hip and urban environment. They also offer a wine bar and tapas that can suit those looking for lighter fare and a good time. This restaurants relaxed vibe and great food makes it a great stop any day.

    Info

  • Høst

    About: Høst, a Copenhagen Food Collective member, is a New-Nordic restaurant renown for its design and its food. They offer fresh takes on New-Nordic Cuisine at an affordable price point. This cozy restaurant located in city center, near Copenhagen University, is a great place to try some unique cuisine while exploring the city.

    Info

  • Torvehallerne

    About: Torvehallerne is covered marketplace featuring stalls with local produce, gourmet foods, beverages and desserts. Located in city-center near the Nørreport St. Metro stop, this is the perfect place to catch lunch while touring the city. It has a variety of selection and offers a place to stock up on whatever you might need during the day while exploring Copenhagen's culinary diversity.

    Info

  • Reffen

    About: Reffen is Copenhagen's dynamic street-food and artisan craftworks incubator. This compound is one of the highlights of Refshaleøen, a former industrial sector in the city's northeastern docklands that has become the place to be for creative startups. Here you can checkout food and products from over 50 chefs and creative artisans while you take in one of the best views of the harbor. Reffen is the perfect pace to immerse yourself in the life of a københavnerne (Copenhagener).

    Info

  • Noma

    About: Denmark's most famous (and probably most expensive) restaurant, Noma is the premier establishment of Chef Rene Redzepi, the pioneer of New Nordic cuisine. This Michelin star eatery offers 20-course meals showcasing the best that New Nordic cuisine has to offer reviving and pioneering techniques and flavors that are uniquely scandinavian.

    Info
