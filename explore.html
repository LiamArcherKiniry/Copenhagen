Where to Go

  • Tivoli Gardens

    Tivoli Gardens is the second-oldest operating amusement park in the world. It offers a variety of rides and beautiful scenery right in the center of copenhagen.

    Info

  • Design Museum Danmark

    Designmuseum Danmark showcases the history of design in Denmark and across the world. The museum offers a collection Danish and international design and the outdoor museum garden and cafe. The perfect place to let your inner designer out.

    Info

  • Christiania

    Freetown Christiania is an intentional community and commune. It's a must visit site when you come to Copenhagen. A great place to wander and soak up the atmosphere, eat some food, buy some crafts, and see some public art. The laws are different here so make sure you abide by the community rules and careful whose photo you take.

    Info

  • The Royal Danish Theatre

    The Royal Danish Theater is home to the Royal Danish Opera, Ballet and Orchestra. Officially the Royal Danish Theatre consists of six stages, all of which are lovely. For a truly amazing night out you should try and see a show at the Gamle Scene. This ornate 19th century theatre is a stunning show in and of itself with ornate decoration, impressive architecture, and a balcony view of the city. Queen Margrethe II is known to frequent it as well.

    Info

  • Rosenborg Castle

    Rosenborg Castle is a Danish Renaissance palace and gardens. It houses a museum where you can find the crown jewels. Perfect if you want to see some Impressive history.

    Info

  • Nyhavn

    Nyhavn is one of Copenhagen's most iconic locals. Nyhavn is waterfront, canal and entertainment district lined by brightly colored 17th and 18th-century townhouses. It's become one of the vistas by which Copenhagen is identified. Almost every building along the canal is a restaurant. It's the perfect place to grab a bite and bask in the beauty of the scenery before walking over to the The Royal Danish Theatre's Old Stage for a show.

    Info

  • More

    Copenhagen has so much to offer it couldn't be summed up in one website. The best thing to do is look for the things that you like. Renting a bike to get to whatever that might be is a quick, fun, and very Danish Way to get around.
